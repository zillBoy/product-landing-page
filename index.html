The Planet Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from Sun and the second-smallet planet in the Solar System after Mercury. In English, Mars carries the name of the Roman god of war and is often referred to as the 'Red Planet. The latter refers to the effect of the iron oxide prevalent on Mars' surface, which gives it a reddish appearance distinctive among the astronomical bodies visible to the naked eye. Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere, with surface features reminiscent of the impact craters of the Moon and the valleys, desert and polar ice caps of Earth.

What is the difference between Earth and Mars? What condition will meet the new settlers?

Mars is a cold and arid planet, which has its own atmosphere, 96% consisting of carbon dioxide, and the minimum temperature drops to -140 degrees Celsius. Every year in the spring on the planet there are dust storms that last from a few hours to several months.

There is no magnetic field on the planet, so the radiation background is increased. And low gravity during a long stay on Mars will make it impossible to return to Earth. A trip to Mars is a one - way ticket to create a new civilization.
